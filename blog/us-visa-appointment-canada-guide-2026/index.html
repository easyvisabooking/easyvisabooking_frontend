US Visa Appointment in Canada (2026 Guide): How People Are Actually Finding Earlier Interview Dates

Published June 2026 · 10 min read · US Visa Canada

You have been refreshing the appointment calendar for days. Maybe weeks. Every time you log in, the earliest available date is months away, or worse, the system shows nothing at all. You have already paid the MRV fee, submitted your DS-160, and done everything you were supposed to do. And now you are stuck waiting, watching your travel plans slowly fall apart while the portal offers you a date sometime next year.

If that sounds familiar, you are not alone. Thousands of people across Canada are dealing with exactly the same thing right now. And while there is no magic shortcut that guarantees an earlier appointment, there are practical strategies that real people are using to improve their chances. This guide covers what is actually happening with US visa appointments in Canada in 2026, how the system works, what most people get wrong, and what you can do about it.

Why So Many People Are Applying from Canada Right Now

Canada has always been a major origin point for US visa applications, but the volume in recent years has been unlike anything the system was built to handle. There are several reasons for this.

First, Canada's temporary resident population has grown significantly. International students, temporary workers, and people on various immigration pathways have all settled in Canadian cities in large numbers. Many of them hold passports from countries that require a US visa for entry, particularly Indian passport holders who make up one of the largest groups of temporary residents in Canada.

Second, cross-border travel between Canada and the United States has always been high. Business travel, family visits, conferences, medical appointments, and tourism all drive consistent demand for US visa interview slots at Canadian consulates.

Third, the post-pandemic recovery of consular operations has not kept pace with the post-pandemic recovery of travel demand. Consulates are processing more applications than they were during the slowdown, but the backlog built up over several years has not been fully cleared. The result is a system where demand consistently outpaces supply, especially in cities like Toronto.

Current State of US Visa Appointments in Canada

The situation with US visa appointment availability in Canada can be described in one word: tight. Wait times for B1/B2 visitor visas have stretched to several months at many Canadian locations. In some categories and for non-resident applicants, the wait can extend well beyond a year.

What makes it harder is the unpredictability. The appointment calendar does not follow a neat, consistent pattern that you can plan around. Some days a batch of new dates will appear without warning. Other times, weeks go by with nothing. Cancellations from other applicants create openings that appear and disappear within minutes, sometimes seconds. If you are not watching at the right moment, you miss it entirely.

You can check current estimated wait times for each Canadian location on the official US Department of State website. Keep in mind that these are estimates and actual availability can differ from what the published numbers suggest.

How the US Visa Appointment System Works in Canada

If you are new to this process, here is how booking a US visa appointment in Canada actually works, step by step.

Step 1: Complete the DS-160

The DS-160 is the online nonimmigrant visa application form. You fill it out on the US Department of State's CEAC website. Once submitted, you receive a confirmation page with a barcode. You will need this confirmation number for the next steps.

Step 2: Create an Account on the AIS Portal

The AIS portal at ais.usvisa-info.com is the official scheduling system for US visa appointments in Canada. This is where you create your profile, manage your application, and eventually book your interview. It replaced the older CGI system (ustraveldocs.com) that some applicants may remember from previous applications.

Step 3: Pay the MRV Fee

The Machine Readable Visa fee must be paid before you can schedule an appointment. For B1/B2 visitor and business visas, the fee is US$185. Petition-based categories like H, L, and O visas are US$205, while E-category visas are US$315. The fee is non-refundable and your receipt is valid for one year from the date of payment. You must schedule your interview within that window.

Step 4: Schedule Your Appointment

Once your fee is processed, the AIS portal allows you to view available interview dates at US consulates across Canada. Select the location and date that works for you. If the only dates available are far out, book the earliest one anyway. This is important for the next step.

Step 5: Rescheduling to an Earlier Date

Once you have a confirmed appointment, you gain access to the reschedule function inside the AIS portal. This lets you check for earlier openings and move your interview forward if something becomes available. Without an existing appointment on record, you cannot access this feature at all. This is why experienced applicants always book something immediately, even if the date is months away, and then work on finding an earlier slot from there.

Most Common Problems People Face

Based on what applicants across Canada are experiencing, these are the issues that come up again and again:

  • No available dates at all. You log in and the calendar is completely blank. No dates for the next several months. This happens frequently at high-demand locations, particularly Toronto.
  • Appointment dates are too far away. The earliest available slot might be 6, 8, or even 12 months from now. For someone who needs to travel in a few weeks, that is effectively the same as having no appointment.
  • Dates disappear instantly. You see an opening, you click on it, and by the time the page loads it is gone. Cancellation slots get snapped up within seconds because so many people are watching the calendar at the same time.
  • Confusion around rescheduling limits. Many people are unsure how many times they can reschedule, whether rescheduling resets their place, or whether it puts their existing appointment at risk. This uncertainty causes people to hesitate even when an earlier date appears.
  • Fear of losing the current appointment. Some applicants worry that if they try to reschedule and the process fails or the system glitches, they could lose their existing confirmed date entirely. This fear, while understandable, often keeps people stuck with appointments that are further out than necessary.
  • Not knowing when new slots open. There is no official published schedule for when consulates release new appointment batches. Without knowing when to check, most people end up logging in randomly and hoping for the best.

Why Toronto Is Often the Hardest Location

If you are trying to book a US visa appointment in Toronto, you have probably noticed that it consistently has the longest wait times of any Canadian location. There are real structural reasons for this.

The Greater Toronto Area is Canada's largest metropolitan region with over six million people. It is home to a massive population of international students, many of whom hold Indian, Chinese, Nigerian, and other passports that require a US visa. It is also Canada's business capital, which means a steady flow of business visa applicants on top of tourist and family visit applications.

The Toronto consulate handles more US visa applications than any other location in Canada. The demand simply outstrips what the consulate can process in any given week. This is not a criticism of the consulate itself. It is a math problem. More people want appointments than there are interview slots available.

For this reason, applicants willing to consider other locations like Calgary, Ottawa, Halifax, or Quebec City may find shorter wait times. Here is a general comparison:

Keep in mind that you may need to travel to a different city for your interview, and you should factor in travel costs and logistics. But if your timeline is tight, flexibility on location can genuinely make a difference.

How People Successfully Find Earlier Appointments

There is no secret trick that unlocks hidden appointments. But there are approaches that consistently give people better results than just logging in once a day and hoping for the best.

  1. Book immediately, then work backward. As mentioned earlier, the single most important step is to book the earliest available date right away, even if it is months out. This activates the reschedule function and gives you a fallback date while you search for something sooner.
  2. Be flexible on location. If you are fixated on Toronto and Toronto only, you are competing with the most people for the fewest relative slots. Check Calgary, Ottawa, Halifax, and Quebec City. The travel cost of flying to a different city is often worth it compared to waiting months longer.
  3. Check at different times of day. New slots and cancellations can appear at various times. People who check only once in the morning miss openings that appear in the afternoon or evening. Checking multiple times throughout the day, while tedious, increases your chances significantly.
  4. Keep your profile complete and ready. When an earlier slot does appear, you need to be able to grab it immediately. Make sure your DS-160 is current, your MRV fee is paid, and your AIS portal profile is fully up to date. Any delay in completing these steps while a slot is available could mean losing it.
  5. Understand that consistency matters more than luck. The people who find earlier appointments are usually not the ones who got lucky on their first try. They are the ones who checked the portal consistently, sometimes for days or weeks, until an opening appeared at a time they happened to be looking. It is a patience game as much as anything else.
  6. Monitor cancellations. When other applicants cancel or reschedule their interviews, those slots become available to everyone else. These cancellation openings are the primary source of earlier dates. The challenge is that they get taken quickly, which is why frequent monitoring matters.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many applicants make avoidable errors that cost them time or opportunities. Here are the most common ones:

  1. Waiting to book until you find a date you like. This is the biggest mistake. By not booking anything, you lock yourself out of the reschedule function. Always book the first available date.
  2. Only checking one location. Limiting yourself to Toronto or Vancouver when other cities have shorter wait times is a missed opportunity. Check every Canadian consulate location.
  3. Letting the MRV fee expire. Your MRV receipt is valid for one year. If you delay too long before scheduling, you risk having to pay again. Start the process promptly after paying.
  4. Submitting an incomplete or inaccurate DS-160. Errors on your DS-160 can cause problems at the interview stage. Some people rush through the form and later discover issues that require them to resubmit, which can delay the entire process.
  5. Panicking and cancelling your appointment. If you are frustrated with a far-out date, resist the urge to cancel. Use the reschedule function instead. Cancelling removes your existing confirmed slot and you may end up with a later date, or no date at all.
  6. Sharing your AIS portal login with unverified third parties. Your AIS account contains sensitive personal information. Be extremely careful about who you share access with. Only work with trusted, verified services if you need assistance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I book a US visa appointment in Canada? ▼

Start by completing the DS-160 form online. Then create an account on the AIS portal at ais.usvisa-info.com, pay the MRV fee (US$185 for B1/B2 visas), and select an available interview date at a US consulate in Canada. You will need your DS-160 confirmation number and MRV payment receipt to proceed.

Can I reschedule my US visa appointment in Canada? ▼

Yes. Once you have a confirmed appointment, you can use the reschedule function inside the AIS portal to move to an earlier date if one becomes available. Keep in mind there may be limits on the number of times you can reschedule, so use this option carefully.

How often do appointment slots open? ▼

There is no fixed public schedule for when new slots are released. Availability changes throughout the day as the consulates release new batches and as other applicants cancel or reschedule their existing appointments. Checking at different times of day can sometimes reveal newly opened slots.

Is Toronto harder than other locations? ▼

Generally yes. Toronto tends to have the longest wait times in Canada due to the Greater Toronto Area's large population, high volume of international students, and significant business travel demand. Locations like Calgary, Ottawa, Halifax, and Quebec City often show shorter wait times.

What is the AIS portal? ▼

The AIS portal at ais.usvisa-info.com is the official online system for scheduling US visa appointments in Canada. It handles account creation, MRV fee payment, appointment booking, and rescheduling. It replaced the older CGI/ustraveldocs system.

Can I move my appointment to an earlier date? ▼

Yes, if an earlier date becomes available. Log into the AIS portal, navigate to the reschedule section, and check for earlier openings. Earlier dates typically appear when other applicants cancel or reschedule their appointments, so availability is unpredictable.

How long are wait times right now? ▼

Wait times vary significantly by location and visa category. For B1/B2 visitor visas, some locations show wait times of several months, with Toronto often being the longest. You can check current estimated wait times on the official US Department of State website.

Is there a faster way to monitor availability? ▼

Checking the AIS portal regularly at different times of day is the manual approach. Some people set personal reminders to check multiple times daily. For those who need consistent monitoring without the manual effort, professional appointment booking services can track availability around the clock and act quickly when earlier dates appear.

What is the MRV fee for a US visa in Canada? ▼

The MRV fee depends on your visa category. For B1/B2 visitor and business visas, the fee is US$185. Petition-based visa categories like H, L, O, P are US$205, and E-category visas are US$315. The fee is non-refundable and valid for one year from the date of payment.

Can I apply at any consulate in Canada if I am not a Canadian resident? ▼

You can schedule an appointment at any US consulate in Canada, but non-residents and third-country nationals often face significantly longer wait times compared to Canadian residents. Some applicants report wait times exceeding a year in certain categories. Always verify current policies on the US Embassy website.

Key Takeaways

Booking a US visa appointment in Canada in 2026 requires patience, preparation, and a willingness to be flexible. The system is not broken, but it is under enormous pressure from demand that exceeds available capacity, particularly in Toronto and Vancouver.

The most important things you can do are: complete your DS-160 and pay your MRV fee as early as possible, book the first available date immediately to activate the reschedule function, check multiple locations across Canada, and monitor the AIS portal consistently for cancellation openings.

Stay informed using official US government resources and avoid relying on unverified information. If your timeline is tight or you need consistent monitoring beyond what you can manage manually, professional assistance can help bridge the gap.

What is FIFA World Cup 2026?

The fifa world cup 2026 is not just another tournament; it is a monumental expansion of the world's most beloved sporting event. This tournament marks the very first time that the World Cup will feature 48 competing nations, representing a major increase from the traditional 32-team format that had been in place since 1998. With 16 additional teams qualifying, the total number of matches will jump from 64 to 104 matches, creating an extended month of football celebration.

This tournament is also historic for its co-hosting structure. While the 2002 World Cup was shared by South Korea and Japan, the 2026 edition will be the first to span three massive countries: the United States, Canada, and Mexico. The geographical breadth of this event means that teams and fans will travel across multiple time zones, climates, and borders. Games will be played in ultra-modern NFL arenas, historic football strongholds, and newly expanded venues, offering a rich diversity of cultures and atmospheres that has never been seen before in a single FIFA tournament.

Why You Should Start Planning Your Visa Now

Securing match tickets is only half the battle. If your nationality requires a visa to enter the United States, the most critical document you need is a valid U.S. visa. The biggest mistake you can make is assuming that a visa application is a quick administrative step that can be handled a couple of months before travel. Here is why immediate planning is essential:

"The surge in international travelers for the World Cup will place unprecedented pressure on U.S. embassies and consulates worldwide. Waiting for ticket draws to conclude before booking your visa interview is a high-risk strategy."

  • Exponential Surge in Demand: Hundreds of thousands of fans from non-Visa Waiver countries will be applying for B1/B2 tourist visas simultaneously. This creates a massive bottleneck at consular offices worldwide.
  • Observed Appointment Backlogs: Wait times for B1/B2 visa interviews are already long at many major consulates. As the tournament draws closer, these waiting lists will grow, and scheduling a slot within a reasonable timeframe will become increasingly difficult.

  • Administrative Processing Delays: Under U.S. immigration law, any B1/B2 application can be selected for administrative processing (Section 221(g)). This process can add weeks or months to your passport return timeline, even if your visa is ultimately approved.

Who Needs a US Visa?

Navigating cross-border entry rules is complex, especially for a tournament spread across three countries. You must analyze your passport nationality, residency status, and exact travel path to determine what documentation is required.

Canadian Residents: PRs, Workers, and Students

A common point of confusion is the travel rules for foreign nationals living in Canada. If you hold a passport from India, China, Nigeria, Pakistan, or other countries that require a visa for the United States, **you must obtain a U.S. tourist visa to cross the border**, regardless of your residency status in Canada. Holding a Canadian Permanent Resident (PR) card, a post-graduation work permit (PGWP), or a Canadian student visa does not grant visa-free entry to the U.S. You will need to book a **us visa appointment canada** through the official portal.

Visa Waiver Program (ESTA) Holders

Citizens of Visa Waiver Program (VWP) countries, such as the UK, Japan, Australia, Germany, and France, do not require a physical visa stamp. Instead, they can apply online for an Electronic System for Travel Authorization (ESTA). However, even ESTA applications can occasionally be denied or delayed (for instance, if you have traveled to certain restricted countries), requiring you to schedule a standard B1/B2 interview instead. VWP travelers should verify their ESTA status well in advance.

Multi-Country Transit Considerations

If you plan to follow your team from a match in Vancouver to a match in Seattle, and then to a match in Monterrey, you will need to meet the entry requirements for all three nations. This means holding a Canadian visa or eTA, a U.S. B1/B2 visa, and checking Mexican entry guidelines (note that Mexico allows entry to holders of a valid, multiple-entry U.S. visa). Keeping your documentation aligned across these borders is essential for a smooth travel experience.

How US Visa Appointments Work

Understanding the standard application workflow will help you avoid simple mistakes that could stall your application. Here is a step-by-step overview of the booking process:

1. Complete the Form DS-160

The DS-160 is the official online application form. Fill it out accurately on the CEAC portal, upload your visa photo, and note down your DS-160 application barcode number. Make sure the info matches your passport exactly.

2. Register on the Official Portal

Create your profile on the official visa scheduling portal. If you are applying from Canada, Mexico, the United Kingdom, Spain, or South America, you will register on the **AIS portal** (ais.usvisa-info.com). If you are applying from India, the UAE, or other parts of the world, you will use the CGI/ustraveldocs portal.

3. Pay the MRV Fee

The Machine Readable Visa (MRV) fee is currently **US$185** for a B1/B2 visa. You must pay this fee using the payment methods accepted by the local portal. This fee is non-refundable and valid for one year. Once processed, your receipt will unlock the scheduling calendar.

4. Book Your Initial Appointment

Log in and select the first available appointment date, even if the date is months away or falls after the World Cup starts. You must have a confirmed slot on record to access the rescheduling interface and monitor for cancellation openings.

5. Monitor and Reschedule

Regularly log into the scheduling portal to check for closer dates. When other applicants cancel or reschedule, their slots are released back into the system. If you see an earlier date, secure it immediately before other applicants grab it.

Tips to Improve Your Chances

If you are managing the scheduling process yourself, these practical tips can help you find earlier dates:

  • Always Secure a Placeholder Date: As soon as your MRV payment is processed, book the first available date, even if it is a year away. Having an active booking is the only way to search the rescheduling calendar for cancellations.
  • Utilize Regional Flexibility: Don't limit your search to a single city. If you live in Toronto, check wait times in Ottawa, Montreal, Quebec City, and Calgary. Traveling to another city for your interview is often worth it if it secures your visa in time.
  • Establish a Consistent Checking Routine: Set aside specific times during the day—such as early in the morning, mid-afternoon, or late at night—to log in. New slots and cancellations can appear at any hour.
  • Avoid Excessive Refreshing: Refreshing the calendar page too rapidly can trigger the portal's security system. This can lead to a temporary account lock, keeping you from booking slots for 24 to 72 hours. Check consistently, but pace your clicks.
  • Keep Your DS-160 Updated: If you need to make minor corrections to your DS-160 after booking, you can fill out a new form and link the new confirmation barcode to your scheduling profile before your interview. Don't let minor errors delay your scheduling process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a US visa if I am a Canadian Permanent Resident (PR) holder? ▼

Yes. Holding a Canadian Permanent Residency card does not grant visa-free entry to the United States. Unless your passport country of citizenship is part of the Visa Waiver Program (ESTA), you must apply for a standard B1/B2 visitor visa and attend an in-person interview at a US consulate in Canada.

Can I attend the FIFA World Cup 2026 with a standard B1/B2 tourist visa? ▼

Yes. A B1/B2 nonimmigrant visa is the appropriate visa for tourism, visiting family, attending sporting events, and short business trips to the United States.

How far in advance should I book my US visa appointment for the World Cup? ▼

You should apply as early as possible. Currently, wait times at many consulates around the world exceed several months, and in some high-demand locations, they can be over a year. The backlog is expected to worsen as the tournament dates approach.

What is the difference between the AIS and CGI portals? ▼

The AIS portal (ais.usvisa-info.com) is the official scheduling system for countries like Canada, Mexico, the UK, Spain, and Colombia. The CGI/ustraveldocs system is used in other regions, such as India, the UAE, and parts of Asia. You must use the portal associated with the country where you plan to take your interview.

What is the MRV fee and is it refundable? ▼

The Machine Readable Visa (MRV) fee is the processing fee charged by the US Department of State. For B1/B2 visitor visas, it is US$185. It is non-refundable, non-transferable, and valid for one year from the date of payment.

What happens if my visa application goes into administrative processing? ▼

Administrative processing (under Section 221(g)) means the consular officer requires additional background information or documentation before making a final decision. This can delay visa issuance by several weeks to months, highlighting the importance of applying early.

Can I apply for a US visa in Canada if I am only visiting on a tourist visa? ▼

Yes, you can theoretically apply as a third-country national. However, US consulates prioritize residents of the country they are located in, and wait times for non-residents are often significantly longer. Additionally, it is harder to prove strong ties to the host country if you are only there as a temporary visitor.

Will buying FIFA World Cup tickets speed up my visa appointment? ▼

No. The U.S. Department of State does not offer expedited appointments or fast-track processing simply because you hold tickets to a sporting event, concert, or holiday. Standard wait times and requirements apply to all tourist applicants.

How many times am I allowed to reschedule my US visa appointment? ▼

Rescheduling policies depend on the local portal rules. On the AIS portal (e.g., Canada), you can generally reschedule several times before your fee is locked, but doing it excessively can prompt the system to freeze your account temporarily. It is best to reschedule only when you find a significantly better date.

Can I travel freely between the US, Canada, and Mexico during the tournament? ▼

Travel between the host nations requires meeting the individual entry requirements for each country. If you hold a passport that requires visas for both Canada and the USA, you will need a valid Canadian visa (or eTA) and a US visa to cross back and forth. Plan your logistics carefully to avoid being turned away at borders.

What documents must I present at my B1/B2 visa interview? ▼

You must bring your DS-160 confirmation page, appointment confirmation letter, a valid passport, a visa photo (if not successfully uploaded online), proof of legal status in your country of residence (like a PR card, work permit, or study permit), and supporting documentation proving your travel purpose, financial stability, and intent to return.

Can I change my interview location after I have paid the MRV fee? ▼

You can change the interview location to another consulate within the same country (e.g., from Toronto to Calgary within Canada). However, you cannot transfer your MRV fee to a different country (e.g., from Canada to India). If you change the country of your application, you must pay a new MRV fee.

Do children require their own US visa appointments? ▼

Yes. Every applicant, regardless of age, must have a complete DS-160 form, a paid MRV fee, and a booked appointment slot. Children under a certain age (often 14) may qualify for an interview waiver, but their profile must still be registered and scheduled in the portal alongside their parents.

What happens if my passport is expiring close to the tournament dates? ▼

As a general rule, your passport must be valid for at least six months beyond your planned period of stay in the United States. If your passport is expiring near the tournament dates, you should renew it before attending your visa interview, or present both the old passport containing the visa and the new passport during travel.
